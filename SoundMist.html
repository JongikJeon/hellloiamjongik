SoundMist
Individual project | 2023
SoundMist provides users with immersive auditory experiences in a spatial context through a novel interaction of spraying sound into the surrounding space. By locating sound in space through sprayed liquid, users experience a blend of different sounds, each generated by distinct liquids. The volume changes as users move closer or farther away, and the sound gradually disperses over time, resembling a fading fragrance.

With technology's progress, we can imagine a future where people could embrace multi-sensory experiences and innovative sound interactions. Inspired by scent diffusion's influence on ambiance, I designed SoundMist to blend auditory experience and spatial awareness, gently dispersing sound for a three-dimensional experience.

SoundMist has been accepted at ACM UIST(User Interface Software and Technology) '23 Poster
Traditionally, dispersing something is linked to tangible materials like liquids or gases, but I foresaw that exploring this as a sound interaction could lead to entirely novel experiences. Furthermore, this interaction could expand into spatial perception, intertwining with the creation of the acoustic ambiance in the surroundings. User testing with the prototype provided further validation for these findings.

SoundMist comprises a spray prototype, an Android mobile app, and a Bluetooth headphone, all connected wirelessly to a server computer. Users interact with the spray prototype to transmit real-time sound identity information, while carrying the mobile app to share their location with the server. With information, the server calculates and delivers the sound to user's headphone, enabling an immersive auditory experience. The whole system is implemented with Python and Arduino.
